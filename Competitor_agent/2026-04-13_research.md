# Wearable Market Research — 2026-04-13

## Key Industry Updates

- **Whoop — $575M Series G & IPO Plans** — Whoop closed a $575M Series G at a $10.1B valuation with backing from Abbott, Mayo Clinic, Cristiano Ronaldo, and institutional investors; CEO Will Ahmed has signaled a potential IPO within two years. The platform is actively adding ECG, blood pressure, AFib detection, and integrated bloodwork — a deliberate push toward medical-grade status that raises the competitive ceiling for all health wearables.
  _Source: [Whoop's Wearable Fitness Tech Lands $575M](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/wearable-fitness-tech-ai-whoop-seriesg-funding/)_ / _[WHOOP Eyes IPO, Glucose Monitoring](https://insider.fitt.co/whoop-eyes-ipo-glucose-monitoring/)_

- **Ultrahuman Ring Pro — Subscription-Free US Expansion** — Ultrahuman launched Ring Pro pre-orders in the US ($479, 15-day battery, ECG + AFib detection, no subscription), directly challenging Oura in a market that drives 60% of global smart ring demand. The no-subscription model adds pricing pressure across the category.
  _Source: [Ultrahuman ramps up US push with Ring Pro as Oura tightens its grip | TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/24/ultrahuman-ramps-up-u-s-push-with-ring-pro-as-oura-tightens-grip/)_

- **Hexoskin Medical System — FDA 510(k) for Continuous Respiratory Monitoring** — Hexoskin's smart shirt received FDA 510(k) clearance for continuous long-term ECG and respiratory monitoring in ambulatory patients, making it one of the first medical-grade wearables approved for out-of-clinic respiratory measurement. Clinical targets include COPD and cardiopulmonary chronic conditions.
  _Source: [Hexoskin Medical System Receives FDA 510(k) Clearance](https://hexoskin.com/blogs/news/a-breakthrough-in-remote-care-hexoskin-medical-system-receives-fda-510k-clearance-for-long-term-ecg-and-respiratory-monitoring)_

- **FDA 2026 Wellness Policy — Broader Leeway for Low-Risk Wearables** — The FDA formalized an updated wellness device framework (announced at CES 2026), giving wearables that measure physiological signals — including respiratory rate — more room to operate without medical clearance, provided all claims remain wellness-only. Regulatory status still hinges explicitly on marketing language and intended use.
  _Source: [FDA's 2026 General Wellness Policy and What It Means for Wearable Manufacturers](https://natlawreview.com/article/fdas-2026-general-wellness-policy-and-what-it-means-manufacturers-wearable-devices)_ / _[FDA exempts more wearable, AI features from oversight | MedTech Dive](https://www.medtechdive.com/news/fda-exempts-wearable-ai-features-guidance/809099/)_

- **Nature Electronics — Smart Acoustic Textile for Respiratory Monitoring** — A peer-reviewed paper in Nature Electronics demonstrated a breathable smart acoustic textile capable of continuous respiratory rate monitoring, distributed tactile sensing, and gesture recognition — using acoustic sensing principles close to Alveos's core technology. Signals growing academic credibility and VC interest in acoustic health sensing.
  _Source: [A smart acoustic textile for health monitoring | Nature Electronics](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41928-025-01386-2)_

- **Consumer Wearables Still Track Respiratory Rate Only During Sleep** — A widely-shared analysis confirmed that Oura, Whoop, and all major PPG-based wearables report respiratory rate exclusively as a nocturnal metric due to motion artifact constraints. Continuous, daytime respiratory monitoring remains an unserved gap in the consumer market.
  _Source: [Respiratory Rate: Your Wearable Tracks It Every Night. You've Probably Never Looked at It. | Medium](https://medium.com/@CuriousCatalyst/respiratory-rate-your-wearable-tracks-it-every-night-youve-probably-never-looked-at-it-c55313f8a26f)_

- **FDA Clears First OTC Cuffless Blood Pressure Wearable (Hilo / Aktiia)** — The FDA cleared the Hilo wristband as the first over-the-counter cuffless blood pressure monitor, establishing the regulatory precedent that previously clinic-only physiological measurements can transition to wellness-tier consumer devices — a pathway relevant to Alveos's longer-term biomarker ambitions.
  _Source: [FDA clears its first over-the-counter, cuffless blood pressure wearable | FierceBiotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/fda-clears-over-counter-cuffless-blood-pressure-monitor-hilo-bracelet)_

## Key Takeaways for Alveos

1. **Daytime, continuous respiratory monitoring is Alveos's clearest competitive moat — lean into it.** Every major platform (Oura, Whoop, Ultrahuman) measures respiratory rate exclusively during sleep, constrained by PPG accuracy. Alveos's mechano-acoustic sensor fusion enables motion-tolerant, continuous daytime monitoring — a gap confirmed in published analysis this week. This should be the sharpest differentiator in Seed pitch decks and all marketing copy: not "we track breathing" but "we're the only wearable that tracks breathing all day."

2. **The FDA's 2026 wellness policy update is an explicit green light for Alveos's gray-zone positioning.** The agency is formally giving respiratory wellness monitoring latitude outside the medical device framework, as long as marketing stays clear of diagnostic claims. Patrick and the team should do a quick audit of all product copy (app, Kickstarter, investor decks) against the FDA's exact wellness language criteria — this also strengthens the CE marking case in Europe.

3. **Hexoskin's FDA 510(k) for ambulatory respiratory monitoring is a useful long-term roadmap, not a threat.** Hexoskin targets COPD in clinical settings; Alveos targets wellness consumers. These don't compete today. But Hexoskin's cleared pathway — ambulatory, continuous, out-of-clinic respiratory monitoring — is the adjacent medical lane Alveos can credibly reference in investor conversations and with clinical partners like University of Kent when discussing the 3–5 year regulatory upgrade path.
