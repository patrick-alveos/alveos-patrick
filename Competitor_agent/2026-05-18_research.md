## Key Industry Updates

- **Ultrahuman Ring Pro now shipping in the US** — After the Kickstarter campaign covered last week, units began shipping May 15 at $399 (no subscription). The no-subscription model is now live in the US for the first time, directly competing with Oura and Whoop on value terms. Post-launch retail pricing is $399 ring + $100 charging case separately.
  _Source: [Ultrahuman Ring Pro vs Oura, Whoop, RingConn — the5krunner](https://the5krunner.com/2026/05/06/ultrahuman-ring-pro/)_

- **Oura Ring 5 roadmap: blood pressure + ECG patents filed** — Credible leaks and patent filings confirm Oura is pursuing blood pressure monitoring from the finger and FDA clearance for ECG. A late-2026 or 2027 launch is expected. If cleared, Oura would expand its biomarker set significantly beyond HRV, sleep, and SpO2 — but respiratory monitoring is absent from all reported Ring 5 features.
  _Source: [Oura Ring 5 investigation: Three features that may debut — Wareable](https://wareable.substack.com/p/oura-ring-5-investigation-three-features)_

- **Edge AI embedding in wearables as proactive health endpoints (May 15)** — Digitimes reports that edge AI is being embedded directly in smartwatches, earbuds, and smart rings, enabling on-device inference rather than cloud-dependent processing. The category is shifting from passive data logger to real-time response system, raising expectations for wearable latency and privacy.
  _Source: [Edge AI is transforming wearables into proactive health and sensing platforms — Digitimes](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260515PD201/edge-ai-wearable-smart-healthcare-2026.html)_

- **Oura + Maven Clinic: wearable biometrics enter clinical care and EHR** — Oura biometric data now flows into Maven Clinic care team dashboards for women's health (fertility, pregnancy, menopause), and Oura is working on broader EHR integration. First major example of consumer wearable data becoming clinical infrastructure, with Oura explicitly targeting physician trust as a growth vector.
  _Source: [Oura, Maven Clinic team up to bring biometric data into clinical care — HealthLeaders Media](https://www.healthleadersmedia.com/innovation/oura-maven-clinic-team-bring-biometric-data-clinical-care)_

- **Hilo wristband: first OTC cuffless blood pressure wearable cleared by FDA** — The FDA cleared Aktiia's Hilo wristband as the first over-the-counter cuffless blood pressure monitor available without a prescription. A landmark precedent showing that novel wearable biomarkers can reach consumer markets via the FDA general wellness / enforcement discretion pathway.
  _Source: [FDA clears its first over-the-counter, cuffless blood pressure wearable — Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/fda-clears-over-counter-cuffless-blood-pressure-monitor-hilo-bracelet)_

- **RingConn Gen 3 ships May 29 — blood pressure vibration alerts, no subscription** — RingConn confirmed a May 29 market launch for Gen 3, adding vibration-based blood pressure alerts, new finishes, and 11–14 day battery. A third subscription-free smart ring entering the market within weeks increases pressure on any device charging a monthly fee.
  _Source: [RingConn Gen 3: A Game-Changer Unveiled at CES 2026 — RingConn](https://ringconn.com/blogs/news/gen-3-smart-ring-blood-pressure-alerts)_

- **"Oura isn't the default anymore" — smart ring market fragmenting (May 15)** — A comprehensive smart ring market review published May 15 concludes that Ultrahuman and Samsung Galaxy Ring have eroded Oura's assumed default status, with competition now spanning price points, subscription models, and feature sets. Market fragmentation is accelerating.

## Key Takeaways for Alveos

1. **Oura is racing toward blood pressure and ECG — not respiratory.** Ring 5 leaks show Oura doubling down on cardiovascular biomarkers. Continuous breathing monitoring (daytime RR, oral/nasal ratio, NSI) remains structurally uncontested by every major smart ring. Alveos should lock in the "continuous respiratory monitoring" narrative now, before Oura eventually expands toward it.

2. **The Hilo OTC blood pressure clearance is the clearest regulatory template yet for Alveos.** FDA cleared a novel wearable biometric at the over-the-counter consumer level via the general wellness / enforcement discretion pathway — the same mechanism Alveos is using. Study Aktiia's regulatory filing approach as a direct analog for how Alveos's respiratory biomarkers could navigate a US market entry without prescription classification.

3. **Smart ring subscription models are under acute pressure — Alveos's €9.99/month needs a sharper story.** With three subscription-free rings (Ultrahuman Ring Pro, RingConn Gen 3, Samsung Galaxy Ring) competing simultaneously, the subscription model requires an unmistakable biomarker moat. RRV, NSI, and continuous oral/nasal detection are the things no PPG ring can do — that's the value proposition to sharpen before the Seed close.
