## Key Industry Updates

- **Ultrahuman Ring Pro returns to US market** — After months locked out due to Oura's patent dispute, Ultrahuman re-entered the US with Ring Pro pre-orders at $349–$399. Oura now holds 85% US smart ring market share. The Ring Pro features on-device ML, titanium unibody, and no subscription — a direct positioning play against Oura's subscription model.
  _Source: [Ultrahuman ramps up US push with Ring Pro as Oura tightens its grip | TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/24/ultrahuman-ramps-up-u-s-push-with-ring-pro-as-oura-tightens-grip/)_

- **FDA relaxes oversight of wellness wearables** — The FDA's 2026 guidance update broadens the "general wellness device" category, distinguishing between "medical information" and "signals/patterns." Wearables providing non-calibrated health readings (heart rate, respiratory patterns, blood glucose trends) for wellness purposes now face lighter regulatory requirements.
  _Source: [What The FDA's 2026 Wellness Device Update Means For Wearables | Hackaday](https://hackaday.com/2026/02/16/what-the-fdas-2026-wellness-device-update-means-for-wearables/)_

- **ARPA-H launches Delphi biosensor program** — The US government's ARPA-H announced Delphi, a 4.5-year program to develop next-gen wearable/ingestible biosensors for continuous monitoring of hormones, cytokines, and immune markers. Proposer's Day is today (March 27). Contracts range $30M–$150M.
  _Source: [ARPA-H launches program to supercharge biosensor device development | ARPA-H](https://arpa-h.gov/news-and-events/arpa-h-launches-program-supercharge-biosensor-device-development-next-generation)_

- **Strados Labs advances acoustic respiratory monitoring** — Strados Labs is running clinical studies with its FDA 510(k)-cleared RESP Biosensor (the "Holter monitor for the lungs") at Jefferson Einstein Philadelphia Hospital, evaluating remote lung sound monitoring for COPD exacerbation prediction. A second study covers cystic fibrosis monitoring.
  _Source: [Strados Labs Announces COPD Exacerbation Study](https://stradoslabs.com/strados-labs-announces-study-evaluating-remote-lung-sound-monitoring-to-predict-copd-exacerbations/)_

- **SonoTextiles: acoustic smart textile published in Nature Electronics** — Researchers published a smart textile using piezoelectric transducers on glass microfibres as acoustic waveguides. Applications include respiratory rate monitoring. The textile is breathable, durable, and stable — demonstrating growing academic interest in acoustic-based health sensing.
  _Source: [A smart acoustic textile for health monitoring | Nature Electronics](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41928-025-01386-2)_

- **Aktiia Hilo Band: first OTC cuffless blood pressure wearable** — Aktiia's Hilo Band received FDA clearance as the first cuffless blood pressure monitor available over-the-counter, launching in the US in 2026. Demonstrates the FDA pathway for novel wellness wearable categories.
  _Source: [Aktiia's Hilo Band Becomes First Cuffless BP Monitor Cleared by FDA for OTC Use](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/aktiias-hilo-band-becomes-first-cuffless-blood-pressure-monitor-cleared-by-fda-for-over-the-counter-use-302501123.html)_

- **Mave Health raises $2.1M seed for neurotech wearable** — India-based Mave Health closed a seed round led by Blume Ventures for its wearable headset tracking neurological signals in real time. Signals continued investor appetite for novel wearable biomarker hardware.
  _Source: [Mave Health's $2.1M Seed Round | Angel Investors Network](https://angelinvestorsnetwork.com/startups/mave-healths-21m-seed-why-neurotechnology-hardware-startups-are-the-new-angel-to)_

- **Stanford SleepFM: AI predicts 100+ disease risks from sleep data** — Stanford researchers developed SleepFM, an AI model trained on 600,000 hours of sleep data from 65,000 participants that predicts risk for 100+ health conditions from a single night's physiological recordings. The team is exploring integration with wearable data.
  _Source: [New AI model predicts disease risk while you sleep | Stanford Medicine](https://med.stanford.edu/news/all-news/2026/01/ai-sleep-disease.html)_

- **Garmin CIRQA Smart Band expected in March** — Garmin is rumored to launch the CIRQA Smart Band imminently, spotted in FCC filings and leaked on Garmin Canada's site. Garmin also pushed software updates to Venu X1, vívoactive 6, fēnix 8 Pro, and Forerunner series in late February.

## Key Takeaways for Alveos

1. **FDA wellness guidance is a tailwind — Alveos's regulatory positioning is validated.** The FDA's 2026 update explicitly distinguishes "signals/patterns" from "medical information," which supports Alveos One's positioning as a respiratory wellness device rather than SaMD. Worth reviewing the updated guidance in detail to ensure marketing language aligns with the new framework, especially as you prepare for CE marking.

2. **Strados Labs is the closest acoustic-respiratory competitor — but targets clinical, not consumer.** Strados's RESP Biosensor is FDA 510(k) cleared and running hospital-based studies for COPD and CF. Their clinical focus (prescription, hospital-attached) leaves the continuous consumer wellness space open for Alveos. However, their clinical validation work could set expectations for what respiratory wearables should prove — Alveos should monitor their published outcomes closely and consider whether the Kent clinical partnership can generate comparable credibility.

3. **The smart ring wars validate wearable health as mainstream — Alveos should position as complementary, not competitive.** Ultrahuman's US return, Oura's 85% market share, and RingConn's blood pressure alerts show the wrist/ring form factor is saturated with HR/HRV/sleep. None do continuous respiratory monitoring. Alveos's differentiation (acoustic sensing, oral/nasal ratio, RRV) is clear, but messaging should frame Alveos One as adding a new dimension ("the fourth pillar") rather than replacing what rings/watches already do.
